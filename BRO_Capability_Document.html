Reading the automation flag

Every capability and lifecycle stage in this document carries an automation flag. The flag is deliberately honest: in a regulated risk function, analytical work can be fully automated, but accountable decisions remain with a named human. Three levels are used throughout.

Autonomous — AI executes end-to-end AI-assisted — AI does the work, human confirms Human-gated — accountable decision / sign-off

“Total automation” in this platform means the entire chain of analysis — intake, scoring, scoping, due-diligence drafting, residual computation, monitoring and reporting — is performed by the system without manual effort, with humans positioned only at the control gates that governance and regulation require.

The automation picture, stated honestly

Across the ten-stage lifecycle, eight of ten stages run with no manual analytical effort — the system intakes, scores, scopes, drafts due diligence, computes residual, contracts, monitors and revalidates on its own. The two stages it does not automate are the two that governance and regulation require a named human to own: the final risk decision and the criticality designation. That is the design intent — total automation of the work, deliberate human accountability for the judgement — every step recorded in a hash-chained, tamper-evident trail.
